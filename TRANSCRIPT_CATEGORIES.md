## 1. Organizational Structure & Governance

### Topics Covered:
- **Onboarding & Training Processes**
  - Need for formal onboarding framework for new members
  - Creating knowledge banks for organizational acumen
  - Cannot rely on external organizations (e.g., fraternities) for structure training
  - Importance of parliamentary procedure and organizational frameworks

- **Leadership vs. Non-Leadership Roles**
  - Defining clear expectations for both leaders and non-leaders
  - Equity in involvement and incentives for all members
  - Recognition that non-leaders keep operations running financially
  - Need to cultivate leadership and mentorship within the network

- **Organizational History & Legacy**
  - Black Alumni Chapter established in 1996 (predates the 90s founding)
  - Lack of honor given to founders and historical context
  - Need to educate members about the organization's affiliation with JSU
  - Many don't understand the network is officially university-affiliated

### Key Quotes:
- "If we as an organization do not have an intro to organization structure framework... we should create a process or training"
- "We don't put any honor to the history. We don't put any honor to the founders"

## 2. Scholarship Fundraising Strategies

### Topics Covered:
- **Competitive Fundraising Model**
  - City-based competition to raise scholarship funds
  - Example: Shannon Shelby vs. speaker in different cities
  - Appears competitive externally but actually collaborative
  - Leverages friendly rivalry (Alpha vs. Kappa dynamic) for fundraising

- **Social Capital Building**
  - More contributions = more leverage with university administration
  - Participation in university events builds influence
  - "It's not always a matter of race. Sometimes it's a matter of money"
  - Building first-name relationships with university leadership

- **Scholarship as Primary Mission**
  - "Whether your event is winning or losing, the scholarship is winning"
  - Focus: Helping Black students get through college
  - Events should prioritize scholarship funding over personal glory

### Key Quotes:
- "All of our money is going towards our scholarship, which gives us social capital with JSU"
- "We need this scholarship to help other black students get through fucking college"

## 3. Geographic Expansion & City Chapters

### Topics Covered:
- **National Network Distribution**
  - Alumni spread across America (Atlanta, Birmingham, New Orleans, DC)
  - Proposal for city-based chapters to maintain continuous engagement
  - Monthly or quarterly events in different cities

- **Benefits of City Chapters**
  - Keeps members tied in throughout the year
  - Creates local community presence
  - Competitive dynamic between cities drives engagement
  - Leads up to annual Black Alumni Weekend

- **Geographic Data Utilization**
  - Using address data to identify where alumni are concentrated
  - Reaching out to pull members together in their regions
  - Recognizing where "heat is shown" among members

### Key Quotes:
- "Establish city chapters... monthly, five months and even quarterly events to keep that engagement going"

## 4. Marketing & Digital Engagement

### Topics Covered:
- **Customer-Centric Marketing**
  - Need to think from "customer perspective" not personal preference
  - "Only age is present" - focus on current engagement tools
  - Different age brackets use different tools - cannot mix strategies
  - Capitalize on where most participation exists first

- **Separate Page Strategy**
  - Proposal: Separate Black Alumni Weekend page from Black Alumni Network page
  - Current confusion: People think network only does the weekend
  - Need to disassociate weekend-only perception from year-round network

- **Social Media Engagement**
  - Manual engagement: Clicking hashtags, commenting on posts
  - Building community through fire emojis, tea, jokes
  - Balancing professional tone with personable engagement
  - Hashtag confusion: #JSU = Jacksonville State AND Jackson State
  - Low follower count (300) in 2021 - need growth strategy

- **Content Strategy**
  - Page only active around certain events - needs consistency
  - Need annual intro content about history and purpose
  - Promoting and giving homage to organizational legacy

### Key Quotes:
- "You don't understand marketing, you're not thinking from the customer perspective"
- "People want to feel like, oh, this is my friend. Oh, that page lit"

## 5. Event Planning & Execution

### Topics Covered:
- **Homecoming & Black Alumni Weekend Merger**
  - 5-year argument to combine events finally succeeded
  - Problem: Naming "Alumni" ostracizes non-graduates
  - JSU graduation rate vs. dropout rate has huge disparity
  - Homecoming only requires attendance, not graduation
  - Splitting events = splitting crowd = poor optics

- **Optics & Marketing Impact**
  - "If this room only holds 100 people and we got 50... it looks lame as fuck"
  - Combined events create better photo ops and energy
  - Creating content for future marketing from successful events
  - Solution: "JSU Black Alumni Weekend + Homecoming" (combined branding)

- **Event Proposal Process (Vegas Greek Picnic Example)**
  - Need formal business proposals for new events
  - Required information:
    - Type of events and execution plan
    - Point of contact and responsibilities
    - Proposed dates, times, locations
    - Complete cost picture (attendance estimates, itemized costs)
    - Hotel rates, airfare estimates
    - Revenue model
    - Photographer/videographer with costs and turnaround
    - Marketing plan
    - Venue policies and restrictions
    - Event insurance costs
  - Must present to exec board → university for approval
  - Cannot use JSU name/trademarks without university approval
  - Need clarity on financial responsibility (network vs. organizer)

- **University Approval Requirements**
  - Cannot "just slap JSU on anything"
  - Need proper channels and approvals
  - This is a university organization, not "straight hustling"

### Key Quotes:
- "What the fuck you thought I was gonna do with a flyer nigga and the idea"
- "We are not planning the event for us. We're planning for the scholarship"

## 6. Data & Analytics Strategy

### Topics Covered:
- **Current Data Assets**
  - Addresses and phone numbers
  - Geographic distribution of members
  - "Scrappy information" - incomplete datasets

- **Missing Data & KPIs**
  - Attendance not systematically tracked ("not like clocked")
  - Scholarship contributions from sponsorships and tents
  - No check-in system for member attendance
  - Cannot identify gaps in geographic coverage or engagement

- **Data-Driven Decision Making**
  - Need to leverage data to identify where members are
  - Track attendance at Black Alumni Weekend 2025+
  - Identify which areas need more outreach
  - Use data to inform city chapter locations
  - Provide "compass" for leaders using available data

### Key Quotes:
- "There's a lot of stuff that we don't have. There's a lot of scrappy information that we work with"

## 7. University Relations & Political Capital

### Topics Covered:
- **Building Political Leverage**
  - Showing up to "red tie functions" and official JSU events
  - Building first-name basis with university president
  - Participation in events that "matter to JSU" beyond the weekend
  - University wants to see more than current efforts

- **University Perspective**

  - Want more robust programming and participation
  - Resource allocation tied to contribution and participation levels

- **Communication Challenges**
  - Leadership (Tyrone) not good at communicating university expectations
  - Need better liaison between network and university administration
  - Misunderstandings about what university expects vs. what network delivers

### Key Quotes:
- "You guys got to participate more in the official JSU things"
- "It's not always a matter of race. Sometimes it's a matter of money and what it is that you are bringing"

## 8. Pipeline & Succession Planning

### Topics Covered:
- **"Cycle of Service" Concept**
  - Create Future Black Alumni Organization on campus
  - Students join as "Future Black Alumni" while in college
  - Mental transition: "Black Alumni is a thing" gets indoctrinated
  - Creates awareness before graduation, not 7 years after

- **Student-to-Alumni Bridge**
  - Partner events between Future Black Alumni (students) and current network
  - Offer incentives:
    - Scholarships for executive body members of student org
    - Networking and mentorship from alumni side
  - Creates legacy feeling and long-term commitment

- **Current Gap**
  - Many alumni don't learn about network until years after graduation
  - Lack of awareness that chapter exists and is university-affiliated
  - No systematic pipeline from student life to alumni engagement

### Key Quotes:
- "I didn't know the black alumni network was a thing until 2021. That is fucking seven years after I graduated"
- "It's indoctrinated and subconsciously planted in my mind to make me move forward"

## 10. Communication & Messaging Strategy

### Topics Covered:
- **Tone & Approach**
  - Balance between professional and personable
  - Let people fully present ideas before critiquing
  - Don't immediately say "no" or "we can't do it"
  - Explain requirements and processes diplomatically

- **Educational Messaging**
  - Clarify that network is university-affiliated
  - Explain approval processes and constraints
  - Communicate scholarship mission clearly
  - Address misconceptions proactively

- **Response Strategy Example (Vegas Event)**
  - Original response was too direct about financing
  - Better approach: "I love the idea... I understand there is some cost... what are your ideas on how we can do this?"
  - Frame requirements as partnership, not gatekeeping
  - Provide detailed needs list to facilitate collaboration

### Key Quotes:
- "I don't want you to feel like if you speak to the network that you're going to get brushed off"
- "Instead of saying no, I might let you talk all the way through it"
